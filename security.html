2. The role of cookies & analytics

We utilize cookies, which are little pieces of software that install themselves on your computer. The information stored in the cookie allows us to tailor your experience and improve our services.

These optimisations include:

  • Special features are activated because the cookie recognizes you as a repeat visitor
  • Recognising your preferred language
  • Remembering your preferences from the previous time you visited the website

We can give you relevant notifications and promotions and make engaging with our site more efficient by tracking this information.

The types of cookies used by UrbanCasino

  • Temporary cookies – these are only stored for the duration of your browser session and are automatically __.deleted when you close it..
  • Persistent Cookies – these cookies remember you across visits, ensuring that your experience with __.UrbanCasino is consistent.
  • Third-party Cookies – these are cookies that send data from another website to be utilized on UrbanCasino's __.website. Google Analytics, for example, embeds its cookies on our site to track your movements.

Cookies do not hold any personal information about you. However, from time to time, we may combine the information from a cookie with your personal information in order to make your experience at UrbanCasino even more personalized.

It's simple to block and delete cookies. However, if you choose to disable cookies on the UrbanCasino website, you will be unable to use many of the features.

One of the services we use that uses third-party cookies is Google Analytics. They acquire data from your device that allows us to track your activities on our site. Your operating system, how many times you visit the site, which pages you visit, and how long you stay on the site are all included in this data. This data is anonymous, but by aggregating it across a large number of players, we can identify areas where we might improve our services to them.

To find out more about how Google uses your information take a look at the Google Analytics Terms of Use and the Google Privacy Policy.
